Last week at hashtag#AWAAI Learn & Lead, Uvika Sharma & I had the pleasure of hosting Soo-Jin Lee, a conversation designer and playwright who’s built human-facing AI experiences across major orgs and brings real storyteller discipline to the work.

Her throughline was simple: prompting isn’t the superpower. Judgment is. The people who get the most out of models know what “good” looks like, and they’re willing to iterate until it’s there.

What stayed with me:

 • 𝗦𝘁𝗿𝘂𝗰𝘁𝘂𝗿𝗲 𝘂𝗻𝗹𝗼𝗰𝗸𝘀 𝗯𝗲𝘁𝘁𝗲𝗿 𝗔𝗜 𝗼𝘂𝘁𝗰𝗼𝗺𝗲𝘀. Soo-Jin showed how building a clear roadmap or outline before prompting leads to stronger, more consistent outputs. AI works best when we give it direction, not just instructions.
 • 𝗔𝘀𝗸 𝗱𝗲𝗲𝗽𝗲𝗿 𝗾𝘂𝗲𝘀𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻𝘀, 𝗻𝗼𝘁 𝗷𝘂𝘀𝘁 𝗳𝗮𝘀𝘁𝗲𝗿 𝗼𝗻𝗲𝘀. Moving from yes/no prompts to exploratory, reflective questions helps AI surface insights rather than generic answers.
 • 𝗨𝘀𝗲 𝗺𝘂𝗹𝘁𝗶𝗽𝗹𝗲 𝗺𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹𝘀 𝘀𝘁𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗲𝗴𝗶𝗰𝗮𝗹𝗹𝘆. Different tools respond differently to creative reasoning, research, and critique — choosing the right model is part of the craft.
 • 𝗔𝗜 𝗮𝘀 𝗮 𝘁𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗸𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗽𝗮𝗿𝘁𝗻𝗲𝗿. Her “digital dramaturg” concept reframed AI as an accountability layer that helps creators iterate, compare drafts, and stay aligned with intent.

We also discussed her play inspired by Hwang Jini, the Joseon-era poet known as “Bright Moon.” Using AI to explore historical voice and narrative structure sparked a larger conversation about identity, authorship, and responsible creative use of generative systems.

AI doesn’t replace human taste, context, or lived experience. It amplifies them. The real skill is knowing what to ask, why you’re asking it, and when to push back on the output.

Huge thank you to Soo-Jin Lee for bringing depth, honesty, and practical insight to the AWAAI community.

Next up: our Feb 28 AWAAI roundtable - more details coming soon.
